pictures of cats

For this project, we were initally given images of small organisms, and explored tools in Adobe Illustrator by tracing them over to replicate their shape and form. From there, we were tasked with creating our custom letterforms using Illustrator, taking inspiration from the images we looked at.
For this project, I first spent time considering the shape and form of the letters, and how to make a series of unique letters fit within a theme. I ultimately decided on the concept of "ocean/marine life" after sketching out some potential concepts. Additionally, this theme kept consistency with the curves and smooth flow of organic life forms, such as the example photos we practiced on.

Following the rough sketches, I began to digitize some of the ones I was pleased with, starting with the beginning of the alphabet. I realized it would also be effective to use similar design elements for different letters to bring more cohesion to the overall set. Using this process, I used Adobe Illustrator to create vector art of the letters, using the brush tool, gradients, and merging forms.

From here, I continued to develop and refine the letterforms according to the rough sketches and theme. For each letterform, I considered their overall cohesion with the others. If a letter felt too out of place, I considered if it would be satisfactory to pair it thematically or visually with another letterform.
For example, the letters A, F, G, and J all share the visual similarity of using a bold line to define the letter and its shape, and then uses other forms, colors, or lines to create an design which ties it to the theme.
Addiitonally, I did not want all the letters to have the same shape and form. I felt this would be too limiting, and would contradict the overall theme, since lifeforms are diverse in form. Many letterforms are curvy and lack sharp edges or straight lines, which I felt did not fit with the playfulness of life and the dynamic nature of the ocean.
From this process, I created the finalized letterform set, which can be viewed below. Many letters share a design theme with one another, and others are tied together by the overall theme of the letterform set.

This was a very challenging project, as I needed to carefully consider how form, color, and lines can create and suggest different letters. But I believe my finished project is a strong first venture into visual design, and I am proud of the work I created.
