The prompt for this project was to take an existing website and make a one-page re-design for it. The tool used for this was Figma.
For this one page re-design, I selected JetPens as my website of choice since it is a site I frequent, and the site in its current state is rather outdated. A screenshot of the current JetPens site can be seen below.

The first step of this re-design was to explore the colors, font, and other inspirations I wanted to pull from when creating the new website design.
I found different fonts using Google Fonts, and used RED, a Pinterest-esque social media site, to find inspiration photos. I browsed and searched for modern and minimalistic websites with visually interesting themes to draw inspiration from as well. Finally, I used Adobe Color Wheel to explore different color palettes for the website.

The next step of my design process was to create different low- to mid-fidelity wireframes. These designs, although simple, were an unique challenge for me to take on. Once I had an idea for what the re-design could look like, I explored different smaller changes it could take, tweaking the elements and layout until I had three different designs. From this, I could get feedback from my peers and evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of each design.
During this step, I also created components for the wireframes, which could be quickly formatted using the Auto Layout tool. Creating these components made it easier for me to make changes to multiple elements later on in the design process.

After getting feedback from my peers and assessing the different wireframes, I began to create the final re-design from a combination of the first and second wireframes.
One of the first major changes I made to the final was the color of the site background. Although the JetPens green color would bring an impactful first impression, I decided it was not user-friendly. Since JetPens is an ecommerce site selling stationery, users would want to see color-accurate product photos. With a green background, their perception of color would be distorted and can result in regretful purchases. Additionally, this color would be straining on the eyes when spending longer times on the site.
Following the change of the background color, the other design changes I made were less extreme. I fixed the sizing for many elements to bring better cohesion to the site.
Following these small changes, I began to edit the components for the finalized version. This involved creating overlay effects and re-sizing elements to better suit the contents of the page. During this step, I also created a login/sign-up popup menu.
The popup menu and components can be seen below.

Finally, I changed the placeholder text into real text taken From the JetPens website, and did the same for images. I then found open-source icons for the navigation, search, and social media icons on the page. Finally, scrolling and clicking interactions were added.
Overall, I believe my re-design reflects the essence of JetPens, but brings a modern look and feel to the site and its interactions. The re-design is still simple, making it easy for customers to navigate and browse.
